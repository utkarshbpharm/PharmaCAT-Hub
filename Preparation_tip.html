2. Time Management Strategies

Daily study approach — 3 phases

Phase 1: Concept Building (0–2 months)

  • Focus on basics — formulas, grammar, reasoning foundations.
  • Study 2 sections/day. Suggested 3–4 hours/day total.
  • Example split: QA 1.5 hr, VARC 1 hr, DILR 1.5 hr.

Phase 2: Practice & Application (2–4 months)

  • Topic-wise problems and past-year questions.
  • Start 1 mock test/week; analyze deeply.
  • Target weak topic improvement each week.

Phase 3: Mock & Revision (Last 2 months)

  • 3–4 mocks/week; daily revision of formulas & vocab.
  • Timed practice to simulate exam pressure.
Tip: sections are time-bound. Practice with section timers to develop pacing.

3. Mock Test Strategy

Why mocks matter: reveal strengths/weaknesses, improve speed, teach time allocation.

How to take mocks effectively

  1. Simulate real exam conditions: same time of day, no interruptions.
  2. Treat every mock as final — no pausing, no extra help.
  3. After-test analysis: log what went wrong, time leaks, and lucky guesses.
  4. Maintain a mistake logbook and revisit it weekly.

4. Section-wise Expert Strategies

VARC (Verbal & RC)

  • Read daily: editorials & long-form articles to improve comprehension.
  • Prioritize RC accuracy before speed; practice 1–2 RCs daily.
  • Para-jumbles & sentence arrangement: 10–15 practice questions daily.

DILR (DI & LR)

  • Practice different DI formats (tables, charts, caselets).
  • Attempt 2–3 mixed sets daily; time each set.
  • In exam: scan all sets, start with easiest; don’t waste >10 minutes on a set.

QA (Quantitative Aptitude)

  • Prioritize Arithmetic, Algebra, Geometry, Number Theory.
  • Create a formula sheet and revise weekly.
  • Solve 30–40 QA problems daily; aim for accuracy over sheer attempt count.
Expert tip: Accuracy matters more than attempts. A few careless mistakes can drastically drop percentile.

5. Mental Preparation & Focus

  • Set realistic progressive targets — aim for steady percentile growth.
  • Consistency > intensity: focused daily practice beats irregular long sessions.
  • Manage stress — exercise, short meditations, and proper sleep.
  • Stay motivated via peer groups or toppers' strategy videos.

6. Expert CAT Preparation Tips

  1. Quality over quantity: master high-yield topics first.
  2. Learn time-saving shortcuts but keep conceptual clarity intact.
  3. Use sectional tests to improve each area regularly.
  4. Analyze every mock — identify patterns in mistakes and fix root causes.
  5. Skip answers you’re unsure of — negative marking penalizes guessing.

7. Last 1-Month Strategy

  • Give full-length mocks every 2–3 days; spend 70% time on analysis.
  • Daily revision of formula sheet, short tricks, and high-frequency question types.
  • Maintain sleep schedule and calm pre-exam routine.

Final Thoughts

Three pillars: Concept → Practice → Analysis. Focus on these consistently and your CAT performance will improve.
